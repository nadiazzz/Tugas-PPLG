Halong Bay

Halong Bay is one of Vietnam's most iconic destinations, located in Quang Ninh Province, about 170 km northeast of Hanoi. Known for its stunning natural beauty, Halong Bay has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1994 and is recognized as one of the New 7 Natural Wonders of the World.

Address: Quang Ninh Province of Hanoi, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Start from 680.000 VND

Hoi An Ancient Town

Hoi An Ancient Town is a beautifully preserved UNESCO World Heritage Site located in central Vietnam. Once a bustling trading port, the town is now famous for its charming lantern-lit streets, historic architecture, and rich cultural heritage.

Address: Hoi An, Quang NAm Province, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 07:00 - 17:00 (The main area)
Price: Start from 120.000 VND

War Remnants Museum

Established in 1975, shortly after the end of the Vietnam War, the museum was originally named the "Exhibition House for US and Puppet Crimes." Over time, reflecting the normalization of diplomatic relations between Vietnam and the United States, it was renamed to the War Remnants Museum in 1995.

Address: Ho Chi Minh City, District 3, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 07:30 - 18:00
Price: 40.000 VND (Adult) / 20.000 VND (Children) / Free (Under 6)

Reunification Palace

Reunification Palace (also known as Independence Palace or Dinh Độc Lập) is a significant historical landmark in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. It served as the residence and workplace of the President of South Vietnam during the Vietnam War and became a symbol of the country's reunification.

Address: 135 Nam Ky Khoi Nghia Street, Ben Thanh Ward, District 1, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 08:00 - 16:00
Price: 65.000 VND (Adult) / 15.000 VND (Children)

Cu Chi Tunnels

Cu Chi Tunnels are an extensive network of underground tunnels located about 70 kilometers northwest of Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. These tunnels were used by the Viet Cong during the Vietnam War as hiding spots, communication routes, and living quarters.

Address: 70 km from Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 07:00 - 17:00
Price: Start from 90.000 VND

Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng National Park

UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Quảng Bình Province, central Vietnam. Renowned for its extensive karst landscapes, ancient caves, and rich biodiversity, the park offers a unique experience for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike

Address: Approximately 50 km north of Đồng Hới city, in Bố Trạch and Minh Hóa districts, Quảng Bình Province, Vietnam.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Free
