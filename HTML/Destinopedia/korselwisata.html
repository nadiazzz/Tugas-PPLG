Gyeongbokgung Palace

Established shortly after the foundation of the Joseon Dynasty in 1392, Gyeongbokgung Palace was the first royal palace built in Seoul. The name "Gyeongbokgung" translates to "Palace of Shining Happiness." Throughout its history, the palace has undergone multiple reconstructions due to damages from wars and occupations. Notably, it was destroyed during the Imjin War in 1592 and later reconstructed in the late 19th century under King Gojong's reign.

Address: 161 Sajik-ro, Sejongno, Jongno-gu, Seoul, South Korea.
Operational Hours: 09:00 - 18:00
Price: 1.000 KRW (Children) and 3.000 KRW (Adults)

Bukchon Hanok Village

Bukchon Hanok Village is a historic neighborhood in Seoul, South Korea, renowned for its concentration of traditional Korean houses known as hanok. Situated between Gyeongbokgung Palace, Changdeokgung Palace, and Jongmyo Shrine, the village offers a glimpse into the architectural and cultural heritage of the Joseon Dynasty (1392–1897).

Address: 37 Gyedong-gil, Jongno-gu, Seoul, South Korea.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Free to walk around the villages

Namsan Seoul Tower

Namsan Seoul Tower, also known as N Seoul Tower, is a prominent landmark situated atop Namsan Mountain in central Seoul, South Korea. Standing at 236.7 meters (777 feet) tall and reaching an elevation of 479.7 meters (1,574 feet) above sea level, the tower offers panoramic views of the city.

Address: 105 Namsangongwon-gil, Yongsan-gu, Seoul, South Korea.
Operational Hours: 10:00 - 23:00
Price: 9.000 KRW (Children) and 11.000 KRW (Adults)

Jeju Island

Jeju Island, located approximately 130 kilometers off the southern coast of the Korean Peninsula, is South Korea's largest island, covering an area of about 1,846 square kilometers. Often referred to as the "Hawaii of Korea," Jeju is renowned for its volcanic landscapes, pristine beaches, and unique cultural heritage.

Address: Jeju island, South Korea.
Operational Hours: Depends on the tour
Price: Depends on the tour

Everland

Everland is South Korea's largest theme park, located in Yongin, Gyeonggi-do. Opened in 1976, it offers a diverse range of attractions suitable for all ages.

Address: 199 Everland-ro, Pogok-eup, Cheoin-gu, Yongin-si, Gyeonggi-do, South Korea.
Operational Hours: 10:00 - 21:00
Price: 59.000 KRW (Adult) / 49.000 KRW (Children)

Haeundae Beach

Haeundae Beach, located in Busan, South Korea, is a renowned 1.46-kilometer-long stretch of sand that attracts millions of visitors annually. Its expansive shoreline and vibrant atmosphere make it a favorite destination for both locals and tourists.

Address: Haeundae-gu, Busan, South Korea.
Operational Hours: 24 hours
Price: Free

Jagalchi Fish Market

Jagalchi Fish Market, located on the edge of Nampo Port in Busan, is South Korea's largest seafood market. Its origins date back to the aftermath of the Korean War, when many women, known as "Jagalchi Ajumeoni," took up selling fish to support their families.

Address: 52 Jagalchihaean-ro, Jung-gu, Busan, South Korea.
Operational Hours: 05:00 – 22:00
Price: Free to enter

Demilitarized Zone

The DMZ is a 250-kilometer-long, 4-kilometer-wide buffer zone that separates North and South Korea. Despite its name, it’s one of the most heavily guarded borders in the world. Tourists can visit locations like the Joint Security Area (JSA), Dora Observatory, and the Third Infiltration Tunnel, where they can gain insight into the Korean conflict and the hope for reunification.

Address: Paju, Gyeonggi-do, South Korea.
Operational Hours: Depends on the tour.
Price: Paid tours, around 50,000 – 70,000 KRW for a one-day tour.

Seoraksan National Park

Seoraksan National Park is one of South Korea’s most iconic natural treasures, known for its dramatic mountain peaks, vibrant autumn foliage, and diverse wildlife. Located in the northeast, it's a popular destination for hiking, sightseeing, and soaking in serene landscapes.

Address: Sokcho, Gangwon-do, South Korea.
Operational Hours: 05:00 – 18:00
Price: 3.000 KRW (Adult)/ 1.500 KRW (Children).

Myeongdong

Myeongdong is a bustling shopping district in the heart of Seoul, famous for its vibrant atmosphere, trendy fashion boutiques, and endless street food options. It’s a hotspot for beauty enthusiasts, offering a wide array of Korean skincare and cosmetic stores. Beyond shopping, visitors can enjoy cultural sites like Myeongdong Cathedral and experience the lively nightlife. It’s a must-visit for anyone wanting a taste of modern Korean culture.

Address: Myeongdong, Jung-gu, Seoul, South Korea.
Operational Hours: 10:00 – 22:00
Price: Free.

Changdeokgung Palace dan Huwon (Secret Garden)

Changdeokgung Palace, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of the most beautifully preserved royal palaces in Seoul. Known for its harmonious blend with nature, it offers a glimpse into Korea’s royal history and architecture. The Huwon, or Secret Garden, behind the palace is a tranquil haven featuring ponds, pavilions, and ancient trees—once reserved exclusively for the royal family.

Address: 99 Yulgok-ro, Jongno-gu, Seoul, South Korea.
Operational Hours:09:00 – 18:00
Price: 3.000 KRW (castle)/ 5.000 KRW (secret garden)

Nami Island

Nami Island is a picturesque half-moon-shaped island located near Seoul, famous for its stunning tree-lined paths and romantic scenery. It gained international fame through the Korean drama Winter Sonata. Each season paints the island in a different charm—from cherry blossoms in spring to snowy landscapes in winter

Address: Nami Island, Chuncheon, Gangwon-do, South Korea.
Operational Hours: 07:30 – 21:30
Price: 10.000 KRW (Adult)/ 4.000 KRW (Children).

Boseong Green Tea Fields

The Boseong Green Tea Fields are a picturesque destination in South Korea, renowned for their lush, rolling landscapes dedicated to green tea cultivation. Located in Boseong County, South Jeolla Province, these plantations produce over 26 hectares of green tea, making it a significant contributor to the country's tea industry. ​

Address: Boseong, Jeollanam-do, South Korea.
Operational Hours: 08:00 – 18:00
Price: 3.000 KRW (Adult)/1.000 KRW (Children)
