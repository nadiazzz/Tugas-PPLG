Kuil Sensō-ji, Tokyo

Sensō-ji Temple, located in Asakusa, Tokyo, is the oldest and one of the most significant Buddhist temples in the city. Built in 645 AD, it was founded to honor Kannon, the goddess of mercy, after a golden statue of her was miraculously found in the Sumida River. Despite being destroyed during World War II, the temple has been beautifully reconstructed and remains a symbol of resilience and spiritual devotion.

Address: 2 Chome-3-1 Asakusa, Taito City, Tokyo 111-0032, Japan.
Operational Hours: 06:00 - 17:00
Price: Free

Fujisan

Mount Fuji (Fujisan) is Japan’s highest and most iconic mountain, standing at 3,776 meters. Located on Honshu Island, it is a symbol of beauty and spiritual significance in Japanese culture. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Mount Fuji attracts climbers, photographers, and nature lovers from around the world.

Address: The border between Shizuoka and Yamanishi, Japan.
Operational Hours: July - September
Price: Start from 3.500 JPY

Universal Studios Japan (USJ)

Universal Studios Japan (USJ) is a world-class theme park located in Osaka, Japan. Opened in 2001, it features thrilling attractions based on blockbuster films and beloved franchises. From exploring the magical Wizarding World of Harry Potter and the interactive Super Nintendo World, to enjoying high-adrenaline rides like The Flying Dinosaur, USJ promises fun for all ages.

Address: 2-1-33 Sakurajima, Konohana Ward, Osaka 554-0031, Japan.
Operational Hours: 10:00 - 19:00
Price: Start from 7.800 JPY (Adult) and 5.500 JPY (Children)

Tokyo DisneySea

Tokyo DisneySea is a unique Disney theme park located within the Tokyo Disney Resort in Urayasu, Chiba Prefecture, Japan. Opened on September 4, 2001, it is inspired by the myths and legends of the sea, offering visitors an immersive experience across its themed ports.

Address: 1-1 Maihama, Urayasu, Chiba 279-0031, Japan.
Operational Hours: 08:00 - 22:00
Price: Start from 8.200 JPY (Adult) and 5.400 JPY (Children)

Shibazakura Garden

Shibazakura, or moss phlox, is a ground-covering plant that blooms in vibrant shades of pink, white, and purple during spring, creating stunning floral carpets across various gardens in Japan. These blossoms typically peak between mid-April and early June, depending on the location.

Address: 2 Chome-7-4 Arakura, Fujiyoshida, Yamanashi 403-0005, Japan.
Operational Hours: 09:00 - 17:00
Price: Start from 300 JPY

Ueno Park

Ueno Park is a spacious public park located in the Ueno district of Taitō, Tokyo, Japan. Established in 1873 on lands formerly belonging to the Kan'ei-ji Temple, it was among the country's first public parks, reflecting Japan's early adoption of Western practices during the Meiji period.

Address: Ueno Park, 5-20 Uenokoen, Taito City, Tokyo 110-0007, Japan.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Free

Fushimi Inari Taisha

Fushimi Inari Taisha is a prominent Shinto shrine located in Fushimi-ku, Kyoto, Japan. Established in 711 AD, it serves as the head shrine for approximately 30,000 Inari shrines across the country. The shrine is dedicated to Inari, the deity of rice, agriculture, and business prosperity.

Address: 68 Fukakusa Yabunouchicho, Fushimi Ward, Kyoto 612-0882, Japan.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Free

Nara Park

Nara Park is a vast public park located in Nara City, Japan, established in 1880. Spanning approximately 660 hectares, the park is renowned for its free-roaming sika deer, considered natural treasures and symbols of the city. According to local folklore, these deer are regarded as sacred messengers of the Shinto gods.

Address: Nara, Nara Prefecture 630-8211, Japan.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Free

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

The Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, also known as the Sagano Bamboo Forest, is a renowned natural attraction located in the Arashiyama district on the western outskirts of Kyoto, Japan. This enchanting grove features towering bamboo stalks that create a serene and otherworldly atmosphere, making it one of Kyoto's most photographed and iconic sites.

Address: Arashiyama, Ukyo Ward, Kyoto, 616-8394, Japan.
Operational Hours: Morning to afternoon
Price: Free

Kiyomizu-dera

Kiyomizu-dera (清水寺, "Pure Water Temple") is a historic Buddhist temple located in eastern Kyoto, Japan. Founded in 780 AD on the site of the Otowa Waterfall, the temple derives its name from the purity of these waters. It was originally associated with the Hossō sect of Japanese Buddhism but became the head temple of the Kita Hossō sect in 1965.

Address: 1-294 Kiyomizu, Higashiyama Ward, Kyoto, 605-0862, Japan.
Operational Hours: 06:00 - 18:00 (July to August), 06:00 - 17:30 (September to May)
Price: 300 JPY (Adults) and free (Children under 6)
