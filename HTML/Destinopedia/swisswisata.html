Matterhorn

The Matterhorn is one of the most iconic mountains in the world, straddling the border between Switzerland and Italy. Standing at 4,478 meters (14,692 feet), it is renowned for its near-symmetrical pyramidal shape. The mountain's four steep faces align with the cardinal directions: north, east, south, and west.

Address: Zermatt, Kanton Valais, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Free

Lake Geneva

Lake Geneva, also known as Lac Léman, is a stunning crescent-shaped lake on the border of Switzerland and France. It’s the largest lake in Switzerland and is famous for its crystal-clear waters, lakeside vineyards, and charming towns like Montreux and Lausanne. Popular activities include boating, swimming, and enjoying the scenic views of the Alps.

Address: Genewa, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 09:00 - 18:00 (Depend on the season)
Price: Start from 30 CHF

Jungfraujoch - Top of Europe

Jungfraujoch – Top of Europe is one of Switzerland’s most breathtaking attractions, located at 3,454 meters above sea level. Accessible via the highest railway station in Europe, it offers stunning views of the Aletsch Glacier and surrounding Alps. Visitors can enjoy snow activities year-round, explore the Ice Palace, and marvel at panoramic scenery from the Sphinx Observatory.

Address: Bernese Alps, Kanton Bern, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 08:00 - 16:00 (Depend on the weather)
Price: Start from 100 CHF

Château de Chillon (Chillon Castle)

Château de Chillon, or Chillon Castle, is a beautifully preserved medieval fortress on the shores of Lake Geneva near Montreux. Surrounded by water and set against a backdrop of mountains, it offers a fairytale-like setting. The castle features centuries-old halls, dungeons, and towers, making it one of Switzerland’s most visited historic sites.

Address: Av. de Chillon 21, 1820 Montreux, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 09:00 - 19:00 (Depend on the season)
Price: Start from 4 CHF (children), 13 CHF (adult)

Zermatt

Zermatt is a picturesque mountain resort town located in the German-speaking section of the canton of Valais, Switzerland. Situated at an elevation of 1,620 meters (5,315 feet) above sea level, it lies at the foot of the iconic Matterhorn mountain

Address: Kanton Valais, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: free

Lake Lucerne

Lake Lucerne, nestled in central Switzerland, is a stunning alpine lake surrounded by mountains and charming villages. Its unique shape and crystal-clear waters make it a favorite for boat cruises, hiking, and sightseeing. The nearby city of Lucerne adds cultural charm with historic landmarks like the Chapel Bridge and Old Town.

Address: Lucerne, Kanton Lucerne, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 09:00 - 18:00
Price: Start from 25 CHF

Bern Old Town (Altstadt)

Bern Old Town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its well-preserved medieval architecture and charming atmosphere. Visitors can stroll through its six kilometers of arcades, see the famous Zytglogge clock tower, and admire the beautiful sandstone buildings.

Address: Bern, Kanton Bern, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Free

The Matterhorn Glacier Paradise

Matterhorn Glacier Paradise, located near Zermatt, is the highest mountain station in Europe at 3,883 meters. It offers stunning panoramic views of 38 alpine peaks and 14 glaciers. Visitors can explore the Glacier Palace, enjoy year-round skiing, and ride the world’s highest 3S cable car for an unforgettable adventure.

Address: Zermatt, Kanton Valais, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 08:30 - 16:00 (Depends on season and weather)
Price: Start from 100 CHF

Montreux Jazz Festival

The Montreux Jazz Festival is a world-famous music event held every July along the beautiful shores of Lake Geneva in Montreux, Switzerland. Founded in 1967, it has featured legendary artists like Miles Davis and Prince. While rooted in jazz, it now celebrates a wide range of genres. The festival’s lakeside setting and rich musical lineup create an unforgettable experience.

Address: Montreux, Kanton Vaud, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 12:00 - 23:00
Price: Start from 60 CHF (Depends on the place and celebrity)

Grindelwald

Grindelwald is a stunning alpine village in the Bernese Oberland, nestled beneath the iconic Eiger mountain. It's a year-round destination offering scenic hikes in summer and world-class skiing in winter. With breathtaking landscapes and charming Swiss vibes, it's perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Address: Kanton Bern, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Free to explore the village

Swiss National Park

Swiss National Park, located in the canton of Graubünden, is the oldest national park in the Alps and a haven for wildlife and untouched nature. Visitors can explore marked trails through rugged mountains, alpine meadows, and dense forests. It offers a rare chance to experience Switzerland's wilderness in its most pristine form.

Address: Kanton Graubünden, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 07:00 - 18:00 (Depends on the season)
Price: Start from 8 CHF

Rheinfall (Rhine Falls)

The Rheinfall (Rhine Falls) is the largest waterfall in Europe, located near the town of Schaffhausen in northern Switzerland. With its powerful cascades and scenic surroundings, it offers a spectacular natural spectacle. Visitors can enjoy boat rides, viewing platforms, and castles nearby for a truly unforgettable experience.

Address: Schaffhausen, Kanton Schaffhausen, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 08:00 - 20:00 (Depends on the season)
Price: Start from 10 CHF (Boat tours) and 5 CHF (Adult)

Lugano

Lugano is a charming city in southern Switzerland, where Swiss and Italian cultures beautifully blend. Set on the shores of Lake Lugano and surrounded by mountains, it offers stunning views and a mild Mediterranean climate. The city features elegant architecture, lakeside promenades, and vibrant festivals, making it a perfect mix of nature and culture.

Address: Kanton Ticino, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Free to explore the city

Gornergrat Railway

The Gornergrat Railway is a historic cogwheel train that has been operating since 1898, connecting Zermatt to the Gornergrat summit at 3,089 meters (10,135 feet). As Europe's highest open-air railway, it offers passengers panoramic views of the Matterhorn and surrounding peaks.

Address: Zermatt, Kanton Valais, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 08:00 - 17:00
Price: Start from 90 CHF

Kunsthaus Zurich

Kunsthaus Zürich is Switzerland's largest art museum, offering an extensive collection that spans over 800 years of art history, from the Middle Ages to contemporary works. The museum features masterpieces by renowned artists such as Claude Monet, Pablo Picasso, and Edvard Munch—the latter represented by the largest collection outside Norway.

Address: Heimplatz 1, 8001 Zürich, Switzerland.
Operational Hours: 10:00 - 18:00
Price: free (Children) and 16 CHF (Adult)
