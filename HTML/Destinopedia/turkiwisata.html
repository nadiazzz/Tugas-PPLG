Hagia Sophia

Hagia Sophia, located in Istanbul, is a magnificent architectural masterpiece with a rich history as a cathedral, mosque, and museum. Built in the 6th century, it features a grand dome and intricate mosaics that reflect both Christian and Islamic influences. Today, it stands as a symbol of cultural harmony and a must-visit landmark in Turkey.

Address: Ayasofya Meydanı, Sultanahmet, 34122 Fatih/Istanbul, Turkey.
Operational Hours: 09:00 - 19:00 (Depends on the season)
Price: Free (Visit the main part)

Topkapi Palace

Topkapi Palace in Istanbul was the grand residence of Ottoman sultans for nearly four centuries. This stunning complex features lavish courtyards, the famous Imperial Harem, and a treasury filled with priceless artifacts. Today, it serves as a museum, offering a glimpse into the opulent world of Ottoman royalty and history.

Address: Cankurtaran, 34122 Fatih/Istanbul, Turkey.
Operational Hours: 09:00 - 18:00 (Depends on the season)
Price: 100 TRY (Adult) and free (Children)

Blue Mosque (Sultan Ahmed Mosque)

The Blue Mosque, also known as the Sultan Ahmed Mosque, is a stunning example of Ottoman architecture in Istanbul. Famous for its six towering minarets and over 20,000 blue İznik tiles, it creates a breathtaking interior. Located opposite Hagia Sophia, it remains an active mosque and a must-visit cultural landmark.

Address: Sultanahmet, 34122 Fatih/Istanbul, Turkey.
Operational Hours: 09:00 - 19:00 (Cannot be visited during prayer times)
Price: Free

Cappadocia

Cappadocia, located in central Turkey, is a magical region known for its unique rock formations, ancient cave dwellings, and fairy chimneys. The surreal landscape is best viewed from a hot air balloon at sunrise, offering unforgettable views. Visitors can explore underground cities, historic churches, and stay in charming cave hotels.

Address: Nevşehir Province, Central Anatolia Region, Turkey.
Operational Hours: 24 Hours
Price: Air baloon tour start from 1500 TRY

Pamukkale (Cotton Castle)

Pamukkale, meaning "Cotton Castle" in Turkish, is a natural wonder famous for its white travertine terraces formed by mineral-rich thermal waters. Located in southwestern Turkey, it's also home to the ancient city of Hierapolis with well-preserved ruins. Visitors can walk barefoot on the terraces and soak in the warm healing waters.

Address: Pamukkale, 20190 Denizli, Turkey.
Operational Hours: 08:00 - 19:00
Price: 80 TRY

Ephesus

Ephesus is an ancient city in western Turkey, known for its remarkably preserved Roman ruins. Highlights include the grand Library of Celsus, the massive Great Theatre, and the Temple of Artemis—one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. It's a must-visit for history lovers and archaeology enthusiasts.

Address: Selçuk, 35920 Izmir, Turkey.
Operational Hours: 08:00 - 19:00
Price: 100 TRY

Troy

Troy, located in northwestern Turkey, is the legendary city made famous by Homer’s Iliad. This ancient site reveals layers of civilizations spanning thousands of years. Visitors can explore the ruins, including city walls and a replica of the famous wooden horse, bringing the myth and history of the Trojan War to life.

Address: Tevfikiye, 17100 Çanakkale, Turkey.
Operational Hours: 08:00 - 19:00
Price: 40 TRY

Basilica Cistern

The Basilica Cistern is an ancient underground water reservoir in Istanbul, built in the 6th century during the Byzantine Empire. With 336 marble columns and atmospheric lighting, it offers a mysterious and serene experience. Highlights include the iconic Medusa head columns. It's a must-see for history and architecture lovers.

Address: Alemdar Mh., Yerebatan Caddesi 1/3, 34110 Fatih/Istanbul, Turkey.
Operational Hours: 09:00 - 18:30
Price: 30 TRY

Grand Bazaar (Kapali Carsi)

The Grand Bazaar (Kapalı Çarşı) in Istanbul is one of the oldest and largest covered markets in the world, with over 4,000 shops. This vibrant marketplace is a paradise for shoppers, offering everything from carpets and jewelry to spices and souvenirs. Its lively atmosphere and historical charm make it a cultural icon of Turkey.

Address: Beyazıt, Kalpakçılar Cd. No:22, 34126 Fatih/Istanbul, Turkey.
Operational Hours: 09:00 - 19:00 (Closed on Sunday)
Price: Free to explore

Bodrum Castle (Castle of St. Peter)

Bodrum Castle, also known as the Castle of St. Peter, is a stunning medieval fortress located in Bodrum, Turkey. Built by the Knights Hospitaller in the 15th century, it overlooks the Aegean Sea and now houses the Museum of Underwater Archaeology. The castle’s towers and sea views make it a fascinating blend of history and beauty.

Address: Bardakçı Koyu, Bodrum Merkez, 48400 Bodrum/Muğla, Turkey.
Operational Hours: 08:00 - 19:00
Price: 30 TRY
